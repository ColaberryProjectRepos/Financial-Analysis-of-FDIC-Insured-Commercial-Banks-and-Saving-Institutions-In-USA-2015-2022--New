Problem Statement 1

In the year 2023, the failures of Silicon Valley Bank and Signature Banks are a reminder of the Banking system's vulnerability, which were the second and third largest Bank failures in U.S. history following the failure of Washington Mutual in 2008. The data from FDIC shows that the total liabilities of Commercial Banks and Saving Institutions have increased by 53% from the year 2015 to 2022, while equity capital has only increased by 25%. This unbalanced increase in total liabilities resulted in a debt-to-equity ratio of 9.7 at the end of 2022, which is higher than the industry standard of around 2. In addition, this high debt results in a higher debt-to-asset (leverage) ratio which also affects the ability of these Commercial Banks and Saving Institutions to pay off their debts with their assets and raises further alarms. This situation can have serious consequences for their customers, shareholders, and the broader economy.
